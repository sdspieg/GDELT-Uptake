# Visualization 03: Russia vs. Israel/Gaza Share Over Time

## Descriptive Summary

**What this visualization shows:**

A stacked area chart showing the percentage share of coercive discourse attributed to Russia/Ukraine, Israel/Gaza, and other contexts across **49 months** (January 2022 – January 2026).

**Categories:**
- **Blue:** Russia/Ukraine relevant — **78,080 records (22.0%)**
- **Red:** Israel/Gaza relevant — **60,984 records (17.2%)**
- **Grey:** Other contexts — **~61%** of corpus

**Key dynamics:**
- Feb 2022: Russia share peaks at **77%** (invasion)
- Oct 2023: Israel/Gaza surges to **~40%**, Russia drops to **~10-15%**
- 2024-2025: Both stabilize — Russia at ~30-35%, Israel/Gaza at ~15-20%

---

## Interpretive Analysis

**What this means for the research question:**

### 1. Attention Is a Finite Resource

The visualization demonstrates that **global media attention to coercive discourse is zero-sum**:

- When Israel/Gaza dominates (Oct 2023+), Russia's share collapses even as total volume peaks
- Russia never regained its Feb 2022 dominance despite ongoing war and nuclear signaling
- RRLS effectiveness is bounded by attention availability

### 2. The 22% vs. 27% Discrepancy

Earlier analysis (23 months through Nov 2023) showed 27% Russia share. The full 49-month dataset shows **22%**.

**Why the decline?**
- Oct 2023 Hamas attack injected massive non-Russia coercive discourse
- 2024-2025 sustained Israel/Gaza presence dilutes Russia share
- Russia's absolute coverage hasn't collapsed, but its *share* has

### 3. Russia's Share Is NOT Structurally Constant

With 49 months of data, the "27% constant" was period-specific:

| Period | Russia Share | Driver |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Jan 2022 | ~40-50% | Pre-invasion buildup |
| Feb 2022 | **77%** | Invasion peak |
| Mar-Sep 2022 | 50-60% | Post-invasion dominance |
| Oct 2022-Sep 2023 | ~40% | Stabilization |
| Oct 2023-Jan 2024 | **10-15%** | Hamas shock |
| Feb 2024-Jan 2026 | 30-35% | New equilibrium |

### 4. Implications for Reflexive Control

**Challenge:** Russia must compete for attention with other crises
**Adaptation:** RRLS timing should account for attention landscape
**Finding:** RRLS during low-attention periods (Oct-Nov 2023) likely had minimal reach

### 5. The Israel/Gaza Natural Experiment

- **Before:** Russia dominated (~40% share)
- **Shock:** Israel/Gaza captured ~40% share
- **After:** Both stabilized at lower levels

**Key insight:** Coercive discourse attention is additive at total level but substitutive at share level.

### 6. The "Other" 61%

Includes: North Korea, Iran nuclear program, China/Taiwan, generic deterrence language. Confirms GKG captures **global security discourse**, not Russia-specific content.

---

## Key Takeaway

**Russia's centrality has declined from 77% (invasion) to ~30-35% (2024-2025).** The Hamas attack permanently restructured the attention landscape. RRLS effectiveness is now constrained by competition — Putin's red lines must fight for bandwidth alongside Israel/Gaza, Iran, and North Korea.
