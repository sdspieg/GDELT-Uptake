## Interpretive Analysis

**What this means for the research question:**

### 1. The Messenger Is the Same

The structural homology between coercive discourse and "red line" source pools validates methodology:
- "Red line" subset is not biased or unrepresentative
- Both drawn from same media infrastructure
- Researchers using "red line" subset study same conversation at higher semantic resolution

### 2. Aggregators Own the Discourse

MSN and Yahoo dominate both corpora:
- Majority of global audiences encounter nuclear-tinged language through algorithmically curated feeds
- Distribution architecture strips context, flattens editorial judgment
- Maximizes exposure to headline-level threat framing

**Reflexive Control implication:** Moscow doesn't need to control distribution pipeline — just seed signals dramatic enough for engagement-maximizing algorithms to amplify.

### 3. iHeart Exposes Blind Spot

iheart.com (radio/podcast aggregator) ranks #3 in coercive discourse:
- Audio-first platforms are significant amplifiers
- Almost entirely absent from scholarly literature on conflict media framing
- Drops in "red line" set — audio platforms use general deterrence language, not precise political formulations

### 4. Global South Is Present

dawn.com (Pakistan), indiatimes.com (India), menafn.com (MENA) consistently appear:
- Coercive discourse about Russia is genuinely global, not transatlantic echo chamber
- Audiences in non-aligned countries receive same deterrence/escalation framing
- RRLS effects ripple beyond NATO to capitals Moscow is courting as partners

### 5. RT, TASS, Sputnik Absent

Complete absence of Russian state media from top 20 is most consequential finding:
- West generates coercive discourse *about* Russia without direct Russian media input
- Russian signals internalized and autonomously amplified by Western editorial infrastructure
- **Exactly what successful Reflexive Control would produce:** target generates self-sustaining threat narratives

### 6. 2024-2026 Continuity

Extended dataset confirms structural stability:
- Same outlets dominate across full 49 months
- Israel/Gaza coverage (Oct 2023+) flows through identical channels
- No new dominant sources emerged for Kursk/Oreshnik coverage
