# Visualization 13: Russia Share Time Series

## Descriptive Summary

**What this visualization shows:**

The percentage of weekly coercive discourse articles that are Russia-relevant, from 2022-2026.

- **Blue fill/line:** Weekly Russia-relevant share (%)
- **Red dashed line:** 4-week rolling average (smooths weekly noise)
- **Horizontal line at 50%:** Reference for majority share
- **Vertical dashed lines:** Key events

**Key statistics:**
- Range: 4% to 77%
- Highest: Week of Feb 21, 2022 (invasion) at 77%
- Lowest: Weeks in late 2023 (Hamas attack dominance) at ~4-10%
- 2024-2025 average: ~30-40%

---

## Interpretive Analysis

**What this means for the research question:**

### 1. Russia's "Share of Voice" in Coercive Discourse

This metric captures **how much of global deterrence/escalation discourse is about Russia** at any given time. It's a proxy for:
- Western attention to Russian threats
- Media salience of Russia-related security concerns
- The "bandwidth" available for RRLS to be heard

### 2. What Drives Russia Share?

| Event | Russia Share Response |
|-------|----------------------|
| Pre-invasion (Jan 2022) | Rising to 50% |
| Invasion (Feb 2022) | Peaks at 77% |
| Post-invasion decay | Gradual decline to 40% |
| Hamas attack (Oct 2023) | Crashes to 10-15% |
| Post-Hamas recovery | Slow climb back to 30% |
| Kursk (Aug 2024) | Spike to 50% |
| 2025 | Stabilizing at 35-40% |

**Key insight:** Russia share is **reactive to competing events**, not just Russian actions. Hamas attack didn't reduce absolute Russia coverage dramatically, but massively increased non-Russia coercive discourse.

### 3. Implications for Red Line Effectiveness

For RRLS to shape Western perceptions (Reflexive Control), they need audience attention. The data shows:

**High-opportunity periods (Russia share > 50%):**
- Feb-Sep 2022 (invasion and aftermath)
- Aug-Sep 2024 (Kursk)
- RRLS in these periods have larger potential reach

**Low-opportunity periods (Russia share < 25%):**
- Oct 2023 - Feb 2024 (Hamas dominance)
- RRLS in these periods compete with Israel/Gaza for attention

### 4. The Attention Competition Hypothesis

This visualization supports a key finding: **RRLS effectiveness is constrained by attention competition.**

- Even at peak Russia focus (77%), 23% of coercive discourse is about other contexts
- At minimum (4-10%), Russia is almost invisible in the deterrence conversation
- Strategic RRLS timing should account for this attention landscape

### 5. Long-term Trend: Declining Share?

The rolling average suggests Russia's share has structurally declined from 2022 peaks:
- 2022 average: ~50%
- 2023 average: ~30% (Hamas effect)
- 2024-2025 average: ~35%

This may reflect:
- "Fatigue" with Russia-Ukraine conflict
- Emergence of other global security concerns
- Habituation to Russian nuclear rhetoric (diminishing shock value)

---

## Key Takeaway

**Russia's share of global coercive discourse fluctuates dramatically based on competing events.** Red line statements issued during low-share periods may have reduced impact simply because attention is elsewhere. The 2022 "monopoly" on deterrence discourse has given way to a more competitive attention environment.
