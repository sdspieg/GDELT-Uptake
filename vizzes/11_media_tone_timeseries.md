# Visualization 11: Media Tone Time Series

## Descriptive Summary

**What this visualization shows:**

Weekly average media tone (V2Tone) in Russia-relevant coercive discourse articles from 2022-2026.

- **Blue line:** Weekly mean tone score (from GDELT's V2Tone field, first value = overall tone)
- **Green fill:** Weeks with positive tone (above zero)
- **Red fill:** Weeks with negative tone (below zero)
- **Vertical dashed lines:** Key events (Invasion, Mobilization, Hamas attack, Kursk incursion, Oreshnik missile, Trump inauguration)

**V2Tone interpretation:**
- Negative values = hostile/negative framing
- Positive values = positive/cooperative framing
- Scale typically ranges from -10 to +10, with most news content falling between -5 and +2

**Key observations:**
- Tone is **consistently negative** throughout the entire period (never crosses above zero)
- Most negative periods: Feb-Mar 2022 (invasion), Sep-Oct 2022 (mobilization/annexation)
- Slight tone improvement in 2024-2025, but still negative
- Kursk incursion (Aug 2024) shows a notable negative dip

---

## Interpretive Analysis

**What this means for the research question:**

### 1. Baseline Negativity

The persistent negative tone (never positive) establishes that **coercive discourse about Russia is inherently hostile** in global media. This matters because:
- Any RRLS/NTS impact on tone must be measured against this negative baseline
- A "successful" red line statement (from Russia's perspective) might not make tone positive, but could make it *less* negative
- The question becomes: do RRLS correlate with tone *changes*, not absolute levels?

### 2. Event-Driven Tone Shifts

The visualization shows clear tone responses to major events:

| Event | Tone Response | Interpretation |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| Invasion (Feb 2022) | Sharp negative spike (-4 to -5) | Global condemnation |
| Mobilization (Sep 2022) | Sustained negativity | Escalation concerns |
| Hamas (Oct 2023) | Slight improvement | Attention diverted |
| Kursk (Aug 2024) | Negative dip | Ukraine offensive coverage |
| Oreshnik (Nov 2024) | Moderate negative | Nuclear escalation concerns |

### 3. What Triggers Tone Changes?

The data suggests tone is primarily driven by **Russian actions**, not Russian statements:
- Kinetic events (invasion, Kursk) produce sharper tone shifts than rhetorical events
- RRLS/NTS don't appear to independently drive tone (would need RRLS date overlay to confirm)
- This challenges the Reflexive Control hypothesis if rhetoric is supposed to shape perceptions

### 4. What Do Red Lines Trigger?

If RRLS are effective deterrents, we might expect:
- Tone improvement after credible threats (adversaries taking statements seriously)
- Or: tone worsening if threats are seen as escalatory

The relatively stable tone (fluctuating within -2 to -4.5 range) suggests **RRLS have limited independent effect on media framing** — events dominate.

### 5. Attention Competition Effect

The slight tone "improvement" during Hamas attack period (Oct 2023-Jan 2024) may reflect:
- Reduced attention to Russia (fewer articles = those that remain may be more analytical/less reactive)
- Israel/Gaza dominating the "negativity budget" in global media

---

## Key Takeaway

**Media tone about Russia is structurally negative and event-driven.** Red line statements don't appear to independently shift framing — kinetic actions (invasions, incursions, missile launches) drive tone changes. This suggests RRLS may not be achieving their stated goal of shaping Western perceptions through rhetoric alone.
