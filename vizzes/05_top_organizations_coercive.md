# Visualization 05: Top Organizations in Coercive Discourse

## Descriptive Summary

**What this visualization shows:**

Horizontal bar chart of the **top 25 organizations** mentioned in the **354,185-record** coercive discourse corpus (January 2022 – January 2026).

**Organization extraction:** From GKG V2Organizations field (NLP-extracted named entities).

**Expected organizational hierarchy:**
1. **Military alliances:** NATO (dominant)
2. **Western government:** White House, Pentagon, State Department, Congress
3. **Russian institutions:** Kremlin, Russian Ministry of Defense
4. **International bodies:** UN, UN Security Council, IAEA
5. **Intelligence/Media:** CIA, FSB, AP, Reuters (methodological artifacts)

---

## Interpretive Analysis

**What this means for the research question:**

### 1. NATO Dominance = Alliance-Level Framing

NATO's expected top position reveals how media conceptualizes Russian threats:
- Target is the Atlantic Alliance, not individual states
- Framing reinforces paper's finding: RRLS/NTS designed to fracture collective resolve
- Reflexive Control predicts this — Moscow aims to manipulate NATO decision-making processes

### 2. Western vs. Russian Institutional Asymmetry

White House, Pentagon, and Congress likely outnumber Kremlin and Russian MoD mentions:

**Implication:** English-language media produces coverage *about* Russian threats from Western vantage, not amplifying Russian voices directly.

**Double-edged for Reflexive Control:**
- ✅ Russian signals achieve salience (first objective of influence operation)
- ⚠️ Interpretive frame set by Washington, not Moscow

The Kremlin generates signals; Western institutions generate discourse *about* signals.

### 3. IAEA Confirms Nuclear Energy-Weapons Nexus

IAEA prominence reflects Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant crisis:
- Novel coercion category: instrumentalizing civilian nuclear infrastructure
- Blurs boundary between nuclear *threats* (policy signals) and nuclear *risks* (structural dangers)
- Corpus captures both intentional signaling and broader nuclear anxiety ecosystem

### 4. Wire Services = Methodological Caveat

AP and Reuters appear as artifacts — GDELT doesn't distinguish between organization *mentioned* vs. organization *credited as source*. These indicate media ecosystem structure, not substantive actors.

### 5. The West Talks to Itself

Western institutions dominate organizational landscape:
- Discourse is primarily Western-generated *about* Russian threats
- Russian state media (RT, TASS, Sputnik) absent from top sources
- Consistent with successful Reflexive Control: initial signal seeded, then West amplifies autonomously

---

## Key Takeaway

**NATO dominance confirms Russia's coercive signaling is framed as alliance-level confrontation.** The Western institutional asymmetry reveals the West is generating its own threat discourse — Moscow provides raw material (RRLS), Western institutions provide interpretation and amplification.
